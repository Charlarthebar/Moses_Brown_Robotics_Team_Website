Where Does Robotics Take Place?

The Moses Brown School Y-Lab

Robotics takes place in the Moses Brown School Y-Lab. The Y-Lab is a maker space with materials and tools for creating prototypes to solve problems, and a home for project-based learning in STEM and beyond.

The Y-Lab at Moses Brown is a 5,000-square-foot makerspace designed to foster "Expert Thinking," a skill essential for 21st-century success. Equipped with both high-tech tools like 3D printers and laser routers, as well as low-tech materials, the Y-Lab encourages students to creatively problem-solve, collaborate, and independently explore solutions to complex challenges.
